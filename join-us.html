Opportunities

Different ways to join the work, held to one research standard.

We are most excited by people and teams who can connect a real research question with careful experiments, well-maintained artifacts, and a willingness to contribute back to the open community.

Students and Visitors

Research Students, Visiting Researchers, and Interns

For PhD, master, undergraduate, and visiting researchers working with affiliated mentors. Strong candidates usually bring a clear research taste, implementation ability, and evidence of independent follow-through.

Research Collaboration

Faculty, Scientists, and Research Teams

We welcome collaborations around new methods, evaluation protocols, data resources, and reproducible releases across world modeling, multimodal reasoning, visual systems, and embodied intelligence.

Open Infrastructure

Open-Source Engineers and Dataset Builders

Contributors can help with training and evaluation code, demos, documentation, dataset cards, benchmark maintenance, annotation tools, and release quality checks.

Institutional Partners

Academic Labs and Industry Research Groups

We look for partnerships with complementary expertise, credible data or deployment questions, shared evaluation needs, and a commitment to publish reusable outcomes whenever appropriate.

What We Value

The best fit is precise, open, and technically grounded.

OpenEnvision is built around public research artifacts. We value work that is ambitious enough to move the field and careful enough for others to inspect, reuse, reproduce, and extend.

01 Research Taste

Clear problem framing, thoughtful baselines, and an instinct for questions that matter.

02 Execution

Reliable code, clean experiments, strong writing, and visible progress under ambiguity.

03 Evaluation Discipline

Metrics, protocols, ablations, and qualitative analysis that support real claims.

04 Open Release Mindset

Reusable artifacts, documentation, licenses, model or data cards, and versioned updates.

05 Collaboration Maturity

Respectful communication, clear ownership, and readiness to work across institutions.

06 Responsible Practice

Attention to data provenance, limitations, intended use, and community impact.

Process

Start with a compact proposal, then define the collaboration carefully.

A focused first message helps project mentors quickly understand fit, feasibility, and the concrete artifact that could come out of the collaboration.

01

Signal fit

Choose one or two research directions and explain why your background matches them.

02

Propose the work

Describe the question, expected contribution, needed resources, and likely release artifact.

03

Discuss feasibility

Align on mentors, data access, compute, timeline, authorship, and institutional constraints.

04

Build in the open

Move toward a paper, codebase, dataset, benchmark, model release, or project page.

Proposal Checklist

What to include in a strong first note.

A one-page proposal is often enough. Make the idea concrete, show relevant evidence, and make the required support explicit.

Research direction

World Model, Multimodal Intelligence, Vision Intelligence, or Physical Intelligence.

Contribution

Method, dataset, benchmark, code module, evaluation protocol, or release improvement.

Evidence

CV, research page, prior papers, prototype, repository, demo, or relevant technical notes.

Practical plan

Timeline, expected mentorship, compute or data needs, and openness or licensing constraints.

Contact

Reach out through public project channels with a specific proposal.

The OpenEnvision public hub and released project pages are the best entry points for current artifacts. Formal participation depends on project fit, mentor availability, and the policies of the relevant universities, companies, and partner labs.
